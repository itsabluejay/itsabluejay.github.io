Pigeon Facts

Introduction

Most people who live in cities overlook the pigeons they pass by every day. Folks often regard these birds as pests, nuisances, dirty, and all around undesirable.
This reputation is unfair and undeserved. Modern city pigeons (aka Rock Dove) are humanities own creation- pigeons (as we know them today), would not exist without our influence. Pigeons were first domesticated at least 5000 years ago - at first as food, later to take advantage of their homing ability, and eventually for show and companionship. Today, "wild" pigeons don't really exist in most of the world. The (usually) grey birds you're probably familiar with are feral. The pigeons you see everyday (if you're in a city) are descended from domestic pigeons that either escaped from captivity, or were deliberately turned loose and abandoned.

Domestic and Fancy Breeds

The most common breed of domestic pigeon is the homing pigeon (or Homer). They look a lot like the feral friends you're probably familiar with, as do many other domestic and fancy birds (They're all descended from Rock Doves, after all). You might be surprised at the amount of variety there is between domestic breeds! Over many generations, pigeon fanciers have put a lot of time and effort into selective breeding to bring out all kinds of wonderful features in these birds.

Exceptional birds!

Pigeons played an especially important military role before the invention of radio and other forms of long distance communication. Their job is a dangerous one - enemy soldiers will try and shoot down birds they suspect are carrying messages.
The Dickens Medal is one of the highest military awards that can be given to an animal, and 27 of them have gone to pigeons. One of these birds was named William of Orange, who belonged to the British military, and was awarded the medal for delivering a message that saved the lives of ~2000 soldiers in the Battle of Anaheim, in WW2.
